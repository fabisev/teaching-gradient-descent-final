# Part II: Multiple Modalities

This part introduces gradient descent through several complementary ways of learning. No previous knowledge of machine learning is assumed.

You will meet the idea through pictures, a worked calculation, an analogy, and an interactive activity. The aim is to make the core process feel concrete before you are expected to explain it on your own.

## What this part includes

- a student-friendly introduction to models, parameters, loss, gradient, and learning rate
- a guided worked algebraic example
- a geometric view of gradient descent as movement on a curve or surface
- a physical analogy that connects the algorithm to a downhill path
- an interactive exploration activity
- a beginner-level distinction between batch, stochastic, and mini-batch updates

## Subchapters

1. **Geometric Visualization** introduces the key ideas through pictures and movement.
2. **Worked Algebraic Example** gives a solved step-by-step example of the update rule.
3. **Physical Analogy** gives an intuitive downhill model.
4. **Interactive Exploration** lets learners test predictions.
